Steps You Can Take To Reduce Your Carbon Footprint:

1. Improve the energy efficiency of your home

To stop cooled and heated air from escaping from your home, make sure it is properly insulated and that doors and windows are weather-stripped. Lastly, spend less energy in your daily activities. Purchase appliances that adhere to American energy efficiency requirements, control indoor temperatures with your thermostat, use your air conditioner sparingly, turn off all lights and appliances when not in use, and swap out inefficient incandescent light bulbs for LED bulbs.

2. Purchase organic products

According to the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations, moving to organic farming can help farmers cut their CO2 emissions by up to 66%. Organic systems do use less energy, but they may also need more acreage, which could negate any possible reduction in carbon footprint.

3. Encourage your Regional Farmer

Supporting regional farmers and lowering emissions are only two advantages of buying locally produced food. Since everything is acquired locally, it is possible to reduce both storage and transit of CO2 by making local purchases.
